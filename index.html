Oh, my love, the queen of our domain,
Who thrives on being the adult, yet drives me insane.
A princess you are, with charm so rare,
Except when I pull your hair (you asked, I swear!).

You’re the adult in this circus, my guiding star,
Yet still my princess, as perfect as you are.
Dark chocolate with strawberries makes your day,
While I sneak one and hope you’ll say, “That’s okay.”

Your house is a temple, all neat and refined,
While my socks on the floor make you lose your mind.
You love when I play with your hair, just right,
And then curl up for old movies late at night.

You like your bed cold but cuddle all night,
A fluffy blanket’s your fortress, snug and tight.
Sleeping’s your hobby, spicy food your flair,
Dhaniya chutney on chicken tikka? Oh, what a pair!

Old movies and podcasts, your timeless muse,
With murder mysteries, you’ll never lose.
Quiet spaces, airy and grand,
Big bathrooms too—now I understand!

Coffee without sugar, some milk is fine,
Shopping with agendas? Girl, that’s your line.
An autorickshaw ride in the light rain we’ll share,
Oversized T-shirts, your royal wear.

Ice cream cones and Bihari bhoj,
Sweet drinks? No thanks! That’s not your road.
Adventurous by day, hanky-panky by night,
MILF over granny? You’re always right!

Talking to YOU is my favorite part,
Saying "I love you" warms my heart.
Oh, my darling, my laughter, my light,
Life with you is a glorious delight.
